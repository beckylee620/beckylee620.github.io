every possible* sock

*toe-up, magic loop, two-at-a-time, stockinette, short-row-and-gusset-heel, rib-cuff

instructions

all measurements can be entered as whole numbers or as decimals.

it does not matter whether you use inches or centimeters for your measurements - as long as you always use the same measurement, and you are precise!

  1. using whatever needles and yarn you want to use for your socks, knit yourself a 20-stitch x 20-row swatch
  2. measure a 10-stitch stretch and enter its width into the appropriate box
  3. measure a 10-row stretch and enter its length into the appropriate box
  4. measure (comfortably and snugly, but NOT tightly) the circumference of the middle of the foot you're making a sock for and enter it into the appropriate box
  5. measure the length of the foot you're making a sock for, standing flat, from end of big toe to end of heel, and enter it into the appropriate box
  6. hit "calculate!"
  7. mark down the output on your copy of the every possible* sock pattern and get knitting!
