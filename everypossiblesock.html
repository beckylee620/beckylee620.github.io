every possible* sock

*toe-up, magic loop, two-at-a-time, stockinette, short-row-and-gusset-heel, rib-cuff

instructions

  1. using whatever needles and yarn you want to use for your socks, knit yourself a 20-stitch x 20-row swatch
  2. measure a 10-stitch stretch and enter its width as a whole number or decimal into the appropriate box
  3. measure a 10-row stretch and enter its length as a whole number or decimal into the appropriate box
  4. measure (snugly, but NOT tightly) the width of the middle of the foot you'd like to knit a sock for and enter it as a whole number or decimal into the appropriate box
  5. measure the length of the foot you'd like to knit a sock for, standing flat, from end of big toe to end of heel, and enter it as a whole number or decimal into the appropriate box
  6. hit "go"
  7. mark down the output on your copy of the every possible* sock pattern and get knitting!

note: it does not matter whether you use inches, centimeters, or anything else for your measurements - as long as you always use the same measurement, and you are precise!
